# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three roles share this app:

- **Visitors** — members of the public visiting government offices, applying for entry permits themselves. Varied literacy and tech comfort; using this on a phone, possibly under time pressure before a visit.
- **Approving Officer** — internal staff who review and approve/reject visitor applications.
- **Gate Security (CISF Guards)** — checkpoint staff verifying an approved visitor's identity (face/QR) at the entrance.

The end-to-end job: a visitor applies for a visit → gets approved by an Approving Officer → receives a digital entry pass → gets face/QR-verified at the gate → enters the building, with no manual paperwork or guard intervention needed along the way.

## Product Purpose

Replace manual, guard-mediated visitor entry at government office buildings with a self-serve digital flow: request, approval, and gate verification all happen through the app. Success looks like a visitor moving from application to building entry without needing to explain themselves to a guard, and gate staff verifying identity in seconds via QR/face check rather than paper logs or phone calls.

## Brand Personality

Official and reassuring, and efficient and minimal — trustworthy like a government service, but as fast and frictionless as an airline boarding pass or checkpoint kiosk flow. No mascots, no casual/startup tone, no ambiguity about application status. Every screen should make the visitor's next step and current status unmistakable.

## Anti-references

- Not a playful consumer/startup SaaS aesthetic (bright gradients, mascots, casual copy, marketing flourishes).
- Not a clunky legacy government e-portal (dense bureaucratic forms, tiny text, dated table-heavy layouts, unclear status language).

## Design Principles

- **Status is always unambiguous** — a visitor or officer should never wonder "what happens next" or "what state is this application in."
- **Checkpoint speed** — the gate verification screen in particular must be scannable and legible in seconds, not read.
- **Plain language over bureaucratic language** — clear instructions and status copy, not government-form jargon.
- **One flow, three lenses** — visitor, approving officer, and gate security are the same underlying pipeline viewed differently; design consistency across roles matters more than customizing each in isolation.
- **Trust through clarity, not decoration** — official and credible comes from precision and restraint, not seals, badges, or ornamentation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast, full keyboard operability, and screen-reader support. Given the visitor population's varied literacy and tech comfort, favor plain language, generous touch targets, and icon+text pairing for status and steps wherever practical.
