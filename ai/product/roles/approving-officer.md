# Role: Approving Officer

## Who they are
Internal government staff responsible for reviewing visitor requests directed to their department or host and deciding whether to approve or reject them. They act on behalf of the office being visited and are accountable for who is let in. Unlike visitors, they are repeat users who work through a queue.

## Context of use
- Desktop or mobile, from inside the office, during working hours.
- Working through a list of pending requests, one decision at a time.
- Needs enough context to decide quickly and defensibly, without chasing the visitor for details.

## What they need to see and do
- See a queue of pending requests routed to them, soonest-visit / newest surfaced clearly.
- Open a request and see the visitor's stated identity, purpose, host, and requested time box in one view.
- Approve or reject with a plain-language reason that the visitor will see.
- Revisit already-decided requests (audit/history) without being able to silently rewrite them.
- Read each request's status using the same status vocabulary the visitor sees.

## What they must never do / see
- Never edit the visitor's submitted identity or ID photo.
- Never bypass or erase the audit trail; decisions are logged and attributable.
- Never see, or act on, requests routed to a department that isn't theirs.
- Never issue a pass directly or perform gate verification — approval and verification are separate steps by separate roles.

## What success looks like
Fast, confident, defensible decisions: every request arrives with the context needed to decide, the outcome and reason are unambiguous to the visitor, and the record of who decided what and why stays intact. The officer never has to leave the app to gather information or to justify a call later.

## Design & voice notes
Same restrained, status-first system as the rest of the app — an approval queue is not a dense enterprise dashboard. One clear decision per screen, plain reasons, consistent status language across all three roles. (See `DESIGN.md`.)
